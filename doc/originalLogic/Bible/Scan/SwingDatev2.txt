From the indicators introduced in the note above, I have combined the following two:

Swing Data – ADR% / RVol / PVol / Float % / Avg $ Vol

ATR% multiple from 50-MA

Since I wanted to keep the amount of information on the chart as simple as possible, I am currently using this combined indicator.

For the “Swing Data – ADR% / RVol / PVol / Float % / Avg $ Vol” indicator, I narrowed it down to only the elements I personally wanted to monitor and removed the unnecessary parts.
Additionally, since I wanted the values from the “ATR% multiple from 50-MA” indicator displayed in the same area, I have combined the two.

Furthermore, the existing ATR% multiple from 50-MA indicator was designed to display a dot above the candlestick when it reached “10,” but since I wanted dots to appear in stages starting at “7,” I readjusted it to display dots in five stages.
It is also freely customizable so that dots can be displayed not only at 7–11 but also at 5 or 6.

Note that I generally do not make new entries when the ATR% multiple is 7 or higher.
I try to look for stocks that are breaking out within the range of “3 to 5” as much as possible.
I’ll explain using $ALAB as an example.
First, when the ATR% multiple relative to the 50-MA reaches 7, a light green dot appears above the candlestick.
After that, as the multiple rises, the dot’s color gradually changes from “green → blue,” and when it reaches 10 or higher, it is treated as “Maximum Overheating.”

Note that I do not use red because it personally makes me feel anxious, and even when it reaches 10, it is not a time to take full profits; however, the color settings are customizable. Please adjust them to your preference.

For reference, on the current chart (October 11, 2025), the ATR% multiple from the 50-MA is 0.57, indicating no signs of overheating and a state of convergence.
