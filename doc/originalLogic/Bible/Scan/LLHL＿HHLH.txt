1. Overview and Key Features
This indicator detects pivot patterns in chart structures—specifically
bullish Higher Lows (LL–HL) and bearish Lower Highs (HH–LH)—by simultaneously scanning multiple lengths.

Furthermore, based on “Priority Mode,” it automatically selects the most optimal pattern and plots its structure and breakout/breakdown levels on the chart.

In simple terms, this indicator detects patterns 4 through 8 shown in the image below and displays the Pivot Line and Pivot Price drawn from 6 to 8.

2. Basic Calculation Method
Bullish Structure (LL–HL) - Long Position
LL (Lowest Low)
  Detects the lowest price within the specified period.

HL (Higher Low)
  The structure is completed when the next low forms at a higher level than the previous LL.

Pivot Line (Resistance)
  Detects the highest High that appears between the LL and HL, and uses this as a breakout trigger.

Bearish Structure (HH–LH) - Short Position
HH (Highest High)
  Detects the highest price within the specified period.

LH (Lower High)
  The structure is completed when the next High forms at a lower level than the previous HH.

Pivot Line (Support)
  Detects the lowest Low that appears between the HH and LH, and uses this as the trigger for a breakdown.

3. Multiple Length Scan
While general indicators use only a single fixed Length (e.g., Length = 5), this indicator scans multiple Lengths simultaneously.

Settings: Min Length – Max Length

Mechanism:
　For example, if Min=2 and Max=10,
　it performs 9 calculations in parallel for Lengths 2 through 10.

This allows the indicator to capture everything from short-term minor pullbacks to larger, more significant structural pivots without the need for manual adjustment.

Since multiple Lengths are scanned, multiple valid patterns may be detected simultaneously.
