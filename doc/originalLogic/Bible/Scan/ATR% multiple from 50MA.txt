Formula
Price deviation from the 50-day moving average (%) / (ATR (Average True Range) / Current Price)

What is the ATR% multiple from the 50MA?
This is an indicator that measures “how far the current stock price has deviated from the 50-day moving average”
by dividing it by “the typical range of price fluctuations for that stock (= ATR).”
This allows you to objectively assess “how far the stock price has deviated” relative to the stock's typical price movement (volatility).

In other words, it lets you check whether the price movement is abnormal by comparing “how much the price has risen” to the stock's “usual movement.”


Current Price: $354.82

50-Day Moving Average: $265.39

ATR (14-day): $18.26

The numerator, “Deviation between Price and 50-Day Moving Average (%)”, is:

(354.82 − 265.39) ÷ 265.39 = 0.3370

The denominator, “ATR ÷ Current Price,” is:

18.26 ÷ 354.82 = 0.0515

Therefore, 0.3370 ÷ 0.0515 = 6.54x

This is how the calculation works.
